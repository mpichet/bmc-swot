Environmental scan — Opportunities & Threats

What this is. A classic SWOT has four quadrants. The 9-block rating you just did gives you the Strengths and Weaknesses — an internal x-ray of your business model. This tab is for the other half: Opportunities and Threats, which come from outside your business.

Osterwalder & Pigneur structure that external view around four "forces" that surround the Canvas (pp. 200–211). Go through each one and list things you'd want the team to act on (opportunities) or watch for (threats), with a 1–5 priority.

  • Market forces — shifts in customer needs, new segments emerging, switching costs changing, revenue attractiveness.
  • Industry forces — competitor moves, new entrants, substitute offerings, supplier/value-chain dynamics, stakeholders.
  • Key trends — technology, regulatory, societal/cultural, socioeconomic.
  • Macroeconomic forces — global conditions, capital markets, commodity prices, economic infrastructure.
